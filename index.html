A Bluegrass Beginning

Welcome to Kentucky! We are excited to show you around the brilliant Bluegrass State - from our very own geographic wonders to significant historical sites, along with vast opportunities for entertainment and engagement. Come explore the state renowned for its horse racing industry, prominent sports teams, and even its world-famous bourbon and fried chicken!

Bluegrass is a unique variety of grass that produces small blue flowers when they grow two to three feet tall.

Kentucky exudes its own form of Southern hospitality, so whether you are traveling solo, or with friends and family, everyone will feel welcome. Additionally, if you are looking for a new place to live and work, you will feel right at home. Explore three of Kentucky's major cities, and see firsthand how Southern history and modernity blend seemlessly.

Kentucky offers top-class tourist experiences to visitors from all around the world!

Visit

Red River Gorge

Located within Daniel Boone National Forest, Red River Gorge was formed by the Red River and showcases massive sandstone cliffs and arches. One most famous arch is Natural Bridge.


Visit

Presidential Signficance

Abraham Lincoln started his career as an Illinois congressman, but he was born and raised in Kentucky. Visit his birthplace in Hodgenville, central Kentucky, and see a replica of his childhood log cabin.


Visit

Cumberland Falls

Often called the Little Niagra of the South, Cumberland Falls draws visitors to its natural beauty. Around the time of a full moon, moonbows can be viewed.


Visit

Mammoth Cave

Kentucky houses the longest cave system in the world. Take in the vastness of the wide rotunda, climb staircases all the way to the exit, and trek across nearby trails.


Visit

Chruchill Downs

Enjoy horse-racing? Book a seat for the famous Kentucky Derby, hosted annually on the first Saturday of May. It precedes the Preakness and the Belmont Stakes.
